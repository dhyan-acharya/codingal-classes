FACTS OF LION
Lions, often called the "Kings of the Jungle," actually live primarily in grasslands and savannas. They are the only truly social cats, living in family groups called prides, and boast an incredibly loud roar that can reach up to 114 decibels and be heard over 8 kilometers away.

FUN FACT:The single most surprising fun fact about lions is that a lion's roar is so loud (reaching up to 114 decibels) that it can be heard from up to 8 kilometres (5 miles) away.
diet:Large ungulates: Their main diet consists of hoofed mammals like wildebeests, zebras, and buffaloes.Medium-sized targets: They frequently hunt medium-sized antelopes, impalas, and warthogs.Massive game: Prides occasionally collaborate to take down massive animals like giraffes, hippos, and young elephants.Small opportunistic meals: When large prey is scarce, they will eat birds, rodents, lizards, and tortoise
habitat:Lions primarily inhabit grasslands, savannas, dense scrublands, and open woodlands. They are highly adaptable cats, but they strictly avoid dense tropical rainforests with heavy canopies and extremely arid, waterless deserts.
